What is UCITS?

  • Undertaking for Collective Investment in Transferable Securities (UCITS), established on 20.12.1985
  • Provides common standards across the European Union for open-ended funds* investing in transferable securities such as Shares and Units.
    *Open-ended funds are able to be issued and redeemed at anytime based on Net Asset Value (NAV). NAV is calculated by subtracting the fund’s liabilities from the value of its assets and dividing the result by the number of outstanding fund shares.

Before UCITS

  • During 60s and 70s investment was a hype in the world.
  • However investors did not have the know-how for requirements of different EU Countries; such as regulations, Tax barriers and country specific guidelines..
  • After UCITS, EU member investors are able to invest their funds to different EU member countries.

All Funds which obey the UCITS directives can be called UCITS Funds.
